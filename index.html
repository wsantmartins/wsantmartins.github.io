About

Microscopic systems are at the heart of many emerging technologies, especially those that harness quantum effects to outperform classical approaches. In my research, I am particularly interested in how environmental influences, such as external fields, radiation, and vibrational forces, shape the dynamics of microscopic systems and their quantum behavior. My goal is not only to understand and mitigate unwanted noise, but also to develop realistic theoretical descriptions that account for imperfect isolation in future quantum technologies, always in close connection with the latest experimental advances. I completed my Ph.D. at the University of Tübingen (Germany), where I worked on quantum simulation, open quantum dynamics, and the physics of Rydberg atoms and ions. Recently, I have also been interested in molecular systems and hybrid atomic systems for quantum information.
